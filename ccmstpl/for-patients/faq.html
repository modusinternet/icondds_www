FAQ Search Form


Use the form below to search through some of our most commonly asked and answered questions.


  • There are so many dentists in Orange County, why should I choose you?

    All of us want to be under the care of the most honest and professional doctors. With over 50 years of combined experience in clinical practice, the doctors at ICONIC form a competent team who will do their best to treat all patients to the best of their abilities, and never treat patients based on their insurance. To learn more about the team, go to our About Us page.

  • What are your hours?

    Our office hours are
    Mon-Fri: 9am - 5pm
    Sat-Sun: Closed
    For office address and direction, go to our Contact Us page.

  • What insurances do you take?

    We take most PPO dental insurance as well as Denti-Cal. For a complete and current list of insurance carriers, give us a call and ask.

  • Do I need to come early for my first appointment?

    Yes. Please arrive at least 10 minutes prior to your appointment time. For a new patient’s first visit, give yourself 15-30 minutes to complete all necessary paperwork.

  • What do I need to bring to my first appointment?

    1. Driver’s license, or non-driver state-issued photo ID (or other legally accepted forms of identification).
    2. Current insurance card(s).
    3. When required by your insurance carriers, co-pays and deductibles are due at the time of appointment. If you have no insurance, please be prepared to pay $100 in full at your initial appointment, which includes acquiring digital x-rays; you will pay $75 if you bring existing x-rays—but only when they’re current within the last 6 months and are of good diagnostic quality.
    4. List of current medications, and all other pertinent medical and dental records from the past.
    5. If possible, please arrive with your new patient forms filled out and signed. These forms can also be accessed from the top of this page.

  • How long will my first appointment take?

    It can vary, but please allow 1 to 1 1/2 hours for the first visit.

  • Why do you need to take x-rays on the first visit?

    Many diseases of the teeth and surrounding tissues cannot be seen when the dentist examines the mouth by visual inspection alone. An x-ray examination may reveal cavities and bone loss due to periodontal disease.

  • Do you offer discounts?

    We do not offer discounts, and here is the two-fold reason. After much research and evaluating our local competitive market, we come up with a fee schedule that’s at or below the national average for most dental services. Because we believe our fees are fairly priced, discounting a fair fee on quality work will send the wrong message about our integrity and competence. Instead of discounts, we offer additional value and perks when you pay in full prior to rendering a service. For more details, give us a call. Also, ask about our reduced fee schedule for any active duty, reserve, and retired military service members and veterans.

  • Why do I need to keep coming back to your office?

    It’s without question that prevention is the key to a long term health. Therefore, it’s in the best interest of the patient to come back for periodic check up and “catch things early.”

  • What should I do if require premedication?

    Please be sure to request a prescription from your physician prior to your appointment, or if you are unsure, contact us and we can help.
